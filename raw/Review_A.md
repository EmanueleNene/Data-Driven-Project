Critical deficiencies that must be fixed to get a pass. 

1) Sections 2.2 and 2.5 tell two completely different stories. Section 2.2 says the 3D strain and stress tensors are reduced to scalar von Mises quantities ε_eq and σ_eq, and that this scalar history is then fed into SINDy. Section 2.5 does something else: separate evolution laws for individual deviatoric components. These are two different methods, not two views of the same one. Pick one and stick with it. Either keep Section 2.2 as background for the plastic case in Section 2.6 only, or redo the 3D viscoelastic benchmark on the scalar path that Section 2.2 promises. As it stands, the theory section and the results section describe two different projects.

2) Equation 15 has a typo on the left-hand side, and Equation 28 is broken. Equation 15 has dS_xx/dt on the left, but the right-hand side is clearly the S_xy equation. It should read dS_xy/dt. Please, pay attention to details.

3) Table 2 is too vague. Rename the column to "Second-order Taylor coefficient". Then add the metric that actually matters: the residual of the identified polynomial against the true exponential Voce-Perzyna law, evaluated over the range of ε_p that is actually reached during training. The error against a truncated Taylor series tells me almost nothing about whether the surrogate is usable.

4) The 3D viscoelastic validation does not test generalisation. You write yourself that the deviatoric stress components evolve independently and can be rediscovered one at a time. If that is true, then the combined uniaxial plus shear case is just the superposition of two trajectories that SINDy has already seen during training. The biaxial case adds a small algebraic coupling through the deviatoric projection, but the underlying dynamics is still a set of uncoupled linear Maxwell equations with the same material constants. A near zero RMSE here only shows that the same linear law can be recovered in parallel for several components. This is fine as a sanity check. It is not evidence that the method generalises to new constitutive behaviour, and it should not be presented that way. Think about it!

5) generate_3d_data.py and generate_deviatoric_data.py implement two different constitutive models. The first applies the Maxwell law component by component to the full six-component Voigt vector, including σ_xx, σ_yy and σ_zz, which is not a correct isotropic 3D Maxwell model. The second works in deviatoric space, which is what Section 2.5 claims to use. State clearly in the report which dataset produced Figures 4 and 5, and remove the other script.

6) The 3D viscoelastic result is not reproducible from the supplied code. I can find the 1D viscoelastic script, the plastic notebook, the two data generation scripts, and the utility files but I cannot find the script that performs the component-wise 3D SINDy fit and produces Figures 4 and 5. That script needs to be added. 

7) The claims about orthotropic or coupled extensions are not supported by the benchmark. Section 2.5 says the material is isotropic "for the sake of calculation" but can be extended to orthotropic materials, and later suggests that the framework will work independently of the complexity of the tensor response! The benchmark in Section 3.2 does not establish any of this. It works because the system is linear, the components are uncoupled, and the same scalar law governs every component. Once anisotropy, coupling between components or material nonlinearity enters the picture, the identification problem changes, and nothing in the current experiments shows that SINDy will still cope. Either remove the extension claims or clearly mark them as speculation for future work.

8) The reference list with one reference is not acceptable. 

9) Fix numerical. grammar and notation problems.

10) The standalone 1D Python script is hard-coded in a misleading way. In MAINCODE_simple_visco_sindy.py, the discovered_ode function builds the time derivative as coeffs[0] + coeffs[1]*sig + coeffs[2]*ed and ignores any higher-order terms that SINDy might keep. For the linear Maxwell case with threshold one this gives the right answer, because STLSQ prunes everything except the bias and the two linear terms, but as a general script it is misleading. Add at least an assertion that the remaining coefficients are zero within numerical tolerance before the rollout starts. On top of that, the report says the plastic identification uses SR3, while this standalone script uses STLSQ with threshold one. The notebook does use SR3, so the reported plastic result is not wrong, but the repository does not make clear which figure comes from which file. Every figure in the report should map to one named script or one named notebook cell.
